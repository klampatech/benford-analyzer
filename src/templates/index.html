What is Benford's Law?

Benford's Law predicts that in many naturally occurring sets of numbers, the first digit is more likely to be small (like 1) than large (like 9).

In a dataset that follows Benford's Law:

  • About 30% of numbers start with 1
  • About 18% start with 2
  • About 12% start with 3
  • And so on...

This distribution appears in many real-world data sets like street addresses, stock prices, and population figures.

Why does it matter? If you have a list of numbers and the distribution doesn't match this pattern, it could indicate:

  • Data has been fabricated or manipulated
  • Numbers are from a limited range (like 1-100)
  • Human-made patterns rather than organic data

The Chi-squared test compares the expected distribution against what we observe, giving us a p-value that tells us how confident we can be in the result.
